The 4-Week ML Systems Study Guide 🗓️

🏠 Playbook Home · ☁️ Cloud · 🤖 Edge · 📱 Mobile · 🔬 TinyML

With over 1,000 questions in this playbook, it is easy to get overwhelmed. This 4-week study guide curates a structured path through the core material, taking you from single-node physics up to fleet-scale architectural design.

If you have 30 minutes a day, follow this syllabus.

Week 1: The Physics of Compute & Memory

Goal: Stop guessing about performance and start calculating it. This week builds your “Mechanical Sympathy.”

  • Day 1: The Roofline Model
    • Cloud: The Profiling Crisis
    • Edge: The TOPS Illusion
    • Homework: Memorize the Peak FLOPS and Bandwidth for the A100 and H100 from the Numbers page.
  • Day 2: Precision & Representation
    • Cloud: The Underflow Crisis (FP16 vs BF16)
    • Mobile: The Quantization Quirk (INT8 vs FP16)
  • Day 3: Memory Systems & The KV-Cache
    • Cloud: The Sequence Length Trap
    • Cloud: The Fragmentation Crisis (PagedAttention)
  • Day 4: Hardware Architecture
    • Cloud: The Sparsity Fallacy
    • TinyML: Co-Designing a TinyML Accelerator
  • Day 5: Pipeline Bottlenecks
    • Cloud: The Data Pipeline Stall
    • Edge: The First-Frame Latency

Week 2: Scaling Up (Distributed Systems)

Goal: Understand how to split a 70B parameter model across 1,000 GPUs without the network starving the compute.

  • Day 1: The Memory Wall of Training
    • Cloud: The OOM Error (Optimizer State)
    • Cloud: The Gradient Checkpointing Boundary
  • Day 2: Network Topologies
    • Cloud: The Cross-Rack Stall (InfiniBand vs Ethernet)
    • Cloud: The Oversubscription Choke
  • Day 3: Collective Communication
    • Cloud: The Ring vs Tree Dilemma
  • Day 4: 3D Parallelism (The Master Class)
    • Cloud: The Pipeline Bubble
    • Cloud: Dimensioning the 3D Cube
  • Day 5: Fault Tolerance at Scale
    • Cloud: The MTBF Crisis (Young-Daly Equation)
    • Cloud: The Straggler Problem

Week 3: Serving at Scale & Edge Deployment

Goal: Move from training to inference. Understand queuing theory, batching, and physical deployment constraints.

  • Day 1: LLM Serving Metrics
    • Cloud: The Serving Inversion (TTFT vs TPOT)
    • Cloud: The Black Friday Collapse (Queuing Theory)
  • Day 2: Advanced Batching
    • Cloud: The Serverless Freeze
    • Cloud: The Batching Dilemma (Continuous Batching)
  • Day 3: Disaggregated Serving
    • Cloud: The Disaggregated Serving (Prefill vs Decode splitting)
    • Cloud: The Decoding Bottleneck (Speculative Decoding)
  • Day 4: Mobile & App Store Constraints
    • Mobile: The App Size Limit
    • Mobile: The App Store Model Size Rejection
  • Day 5: Fleet Updates
    • Mobile: The Cross-Version Compatibility Maze
    • TinyML: The FOTA Update Risk

Week 4: Ops, Power, and the “Real World”

Goal: Answer the questions that separate standard engineers from technical leads. Security, thermal physics, and silent failures.

  • Day 1: Thermal & Power Budgets
    • Cloud: The Rack Power Budget
    • Cloud: The Thermal Throttling Mystery
    • Mobile: The Sustained vs Burst Reality
  • Day 2: Monitoring & Silent Failures
    • Cloud: The GPU Utilization Paradox
    • Mobile: The Silent Accuracy Degradation
  • Day 3: On-Device Architectures
    • TinyML: Sub-Milliwatt Always-On Wake Word Detection
    • Mobile: The 50-Feature Mobile ML Platform
  • Day 4: Security, Privacy & Silent Failures
    • Cloud: The Tokenizer Mismatch
    • Mobile: The Federated Keyboard (DP-FedAvg)
  • Day 5: Visual Architecture Debugging
    • Take the Ultimate Test: Try to solve the architectures in Cloud Visual Debugging without looking at the answers.
