Programs

The AI Engineering Community runs four programs that bring the curriculum to life beyond the textbook.

Global Network

TinyML4D Academic Network

A working group focused on enabling ML systems education in developing countries. Faculty from partner institutions receive training, hardware kits, and curriculum materials. Coordinated through ICTP in Trieste, Italy.

40+ universities · 6 continents · Co-chaired by Marco Zennaro (ICTP) & Marcelo Rovai (UNIFEI)
Annual Workshop

SciTinyML

Annual scientific workshop series at the International Centre for Theoretical Physics (ICTP) demonstrating how ML can be applied to real-world scientific challenges — from environmental monitoring to health diagnostics — in resource-constrained settings.

Trieste, Italy · Running since 2021 · Hands-on hardware labs included
Monthly Showcase

Show & Tell

A monthly community showcase where researchers and students present ML projects, share results, and receive feedback from the global community. Open to all skill levels.

Monthly · Virtual · Chaired by Jeremy Ellis
Online Certificate

edX Professional Certificate

The HarvardX TinyML Professional Certificate on edX — a series of courses covering the fundamentals of machine learning on embedded devices. Completed by thousands of learners worldwide.

Self-paced · edX platform · Instructors: Reddi, Moroney, Warden, Suzuki

Global Network

AI engineering education should not be limited by geography or resources. Through the TinyML4D Academic Network and partnerships with ICTP, the tinyML Foundation, and global universities, we are building infrastructure for accessible, high-quality ML systems education worldwide.

Research

The community contributes to applied research across multiple domains, with publications in IEEE, ACM, and scientific journals.

  • Environmental monitoring — water leakage detection, animal tracking, sustainability assessment
  • Healthcare — atrial fibrillation detection, disease classification on microcontrollers
  • Smart infrastructure — predictive maintenance, indoor localization, asset tracking
  • Education — scaling ML training in developing regions, curriculum design
  • Benchmarking — MLPerf Tiny, energy-efficient inference measurement

For the full publication list, see the research page on tinyml.seas.harvard.edu.
